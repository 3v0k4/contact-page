Insulin and food is a matching game..

..And you will be wrong on either dosing or timing. Most likely, both.

This is what happens to your blood sugars with a carb heavy meal.

To match that amount of carbs you need a high dose of rapid insulin.

Except, you will be wrong timing insulin. For example, you could bolus too early.

Or, you could be wrong dosing insulin. Like an over bolus.

Most likely, you will be wrong both timing and dosing insulin.

And yeah, those lows look dangerous! But what happens if you eat less carbs and more proteins?

Wow, that takes a long time and it's a tiny rise. Let's add some insulin, shall we?

And now, let's be wrong with both timing and dosing with a lower-carb meal.

Screwed up both timing and dosing. Still you got a tiny bit out of range and had all the time to catch it.

No doubts, insulin management with less carbs* is way more forgiving.

* Less carbs does not necessarily mean keto nor super low-carb. Consult a real doctor (not me) before making any changes.
